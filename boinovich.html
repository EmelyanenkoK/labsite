Ludmila B. Boinovich

Prof. Ludmila Borisovna Boinovich

The scientific career of Ludmila Boinovich began in the 1980s in the laboratory of famous Russian scientist B.V. Derjaguin. At the beginning of her scientific carrier her research activities, both theoretical and experimental, have been centered on molecular physics and the physical chemistry of surface phenomena. She received a Ph.D. in Physical Chemistry at the Institute of Physical Chemistry, Academy of Sciences of USSR in 1987, and a Dr. of Sciences in Physics and Mathematics in 2004. In 2006 Prof. L. Boinovich was elected a Corresponding Member and in 2016- a full Member of the Russian Academy of Sciences. In 2009, she assumed the post of Head of the Scientific and Educational Center on Colloid Chemistry and Surface Phenomena. Currently, the research activities of Ludmila Boinovich focus on surface phenomena, surface forces, wetting, the design of functional coatings, and the development of the fundamentals of protection against atmospheric icing, corrosion, and bio-contamination. She had authored more than 200 papers. Her studies on the design of functional coatings and properties of materials with extreme wettability led to the creation of efficient materials for the power industry, aviation, and mechanical engineering. For studies on materials with extreme wettability, she was awarded the Gold medal of the Institute of European Community (ICEPEC), the prize of the World Intellectual Property Organization for the best woman inventor of the year.
